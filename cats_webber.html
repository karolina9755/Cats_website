About the composer

Andrew Lloyd Webber (born March 22, 1948) is a British composer known for his significant contributions to musical theatre. He is responsible for some of the most successful musicals of all time, including Cats, The Phantom of the Opera, and Evita.

Webber's music is known for its memorable melodies and grand orchestration. Over his career, he has won numerous awards, including multiple Tony Awards, Olivier Awards, and an Academy Award.

His work on Cats adapted T.S. Eliot''s Old Possum's Book of Practical Cats into a full-length musical, with the addition of the iconic song Memory, which became one of the most famous songs in musical theatre history.

For more about Andrew Lloyd Webber's work, visit his official website: andrewlloydwebber.com.
